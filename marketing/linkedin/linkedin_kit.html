The voice note.
~50 seconds spoken. The script lives in the markdown file. Three rules: smile while talking, name + dealership in the first 5 sec, single ask ("shoot me a thumbs up").
The script · 50 sec
"Hey [FIRST_NAME], John Roberts here, House of Carz in Rochester, Indiana. Real quick — I won't waste your time.

I built an AI BDC at our store after I got tired of watching 9pm Saturday leads die in the inbox. Three channels — voice, text, email — answers in under a minute, 24/7. Built it for our lot, now I'm sharing it with other indies.

If you're curious, just shoot me a thumbs up here and I'll send over a 90-second walkthrough. No pitch deck, no sales call.

Either way, appreciate the add. Have a good one."
PRO TIPS
  • → Smile while you talk — changes your tone
  • → Name + dealership in first 5 sec
  • → End with a single ask
  • → Rehearse once, then sound natural

A
Profile first, then DM. Before sending the opener, spend 30 sec on their profile. Find ONE specific detail (recent post, a vehicle they featured, an anniversary). Swap the generic story setup for "Saw your post about [X] — reminded me of something..." — this doubles reply rates.
B
Don't sell from #02. The opener's only goal is a YES to "want to see it?", not a closed deal. The pitch goes in #03 — AFTER they've leaned in. Selling in the opener is why most cold DMs get archived.
