## Summary

Toma positions itself as an "AI operating system" for dealership conversations rather than a single-channel tool. It layers on top of existing CRM/DMS/AI investments to coordinate conversations, tasks, and decisions across sales, BDC, and service.

## Market Position

- Voice AI agents specifically for automotive dealerships.
- Answer every inbound call.
- Automate service scheduling.
- Dealer-specific safeguards to protect revenue.
- Used by 100+ dealerships including Lithia Motors, AutoNation, and Car Dealership Guy's operations.
- Backed by a16z (Andreessen Horowitz funding, TechCrunch June 2025).
- Graduated from Y Combinator.

## Performance Metrics

- **Appointment booking:** 100+ extra appointments per month per dealership.
- **Advisor time saved:** 30-40 hours/week, up to 43 hours/month per dealership.
- **BDC workload reduction:** 40%.
- **Resolution rate:** 50-80% of calls handled end-to-end without human transfer.
- **Case study (Martin Management Group):** 9,000 service appointments booked in 90 days.
- **Inbound call coverage:** 100% answered.

## Architecture Philosophy

Toma's differentiator: rather than replacing CRMs, DMSs, or existing AI investments, it acts as the "intelligent layer" that interconnects them. One unified AI "brain" coordinates conversations, tasks, and decisions across departments.

## Safeguards

The platform emphasizes "dealer-specific safeguards to protect revenue." This is likely a response to incidents like the Chevy $1 Tahoe chatbot fiasco — guardrails that prevent AI from agreeing to unauthorized prices, illegal claims, or off-brand commitments.
