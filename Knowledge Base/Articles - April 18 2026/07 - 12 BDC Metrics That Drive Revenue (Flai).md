## Summary

The essential BDC performance metrics every dealership should be tracking, with industry benchmarks for each.

## Key BDC Metrics and Benchmarks

### 1. Appointment Set Rate (ASR)
- Percentage of contacted leads that result in a scheduled appointment.
- **Phone leads:** 74% set rate (April 2025 industry data).
- **Internet leads:** 40% set rate.
- Phone leads nearly 2x more effective at setting appointments.

### 2. Appointment Show Rate
- Percentage of set appointments that actually show up.
- **Industry benchmark:** 50-65% of appointments no-show.
- **Top-performer target:** 80% show rate.
- Combined show-to-sale conversion from all lead sources: ~41%.

### 3. Close Rate
- Percentage of ups/opportunities that result in a sale.
- **Industry average:** 3-5%.
- **Target for dealerships:** 10-15%.
- **Top performers:** 10%+.

### 4. Lead Response Time (Speed to Lead)
- Time from lead inquiry to first meaningful contact.
- **Industry leader target:** Under 5 minutes for digital, under 30 seconds for phone.
- **78% of customers buy from the dealer that responds first.**
- Responding within 5 minutes = 7x more likely to connect.

### 5. Lead-to-Appointment Conversion
- Similar to ASR but across all lead sources.
- Benchmark: industry varies by lead source (web forms lower, phone higher).

### 6. Appointment-to-Sale Rate
- Percentage of kept appointments that result in a sale.
- Combined with show rate gives full funnel visibility.

### 7. Contact Rate
- Percentage of leads that can actually be reached (valid phone/email).
- AI follow-up can significantly improve this metric through persistence.

### 8. Cost per Acquired Customer (CAC)
- Marketing spend / sales generated.
- BDC efficiency directly impacts this number.

### 9. First-Call Resolution Rate
- Percentage of calls resolved without transfer or callback.
- Target for service departments: >70%.

### 10. Customer Satisfaction Index (CSI)
- Post-transaction satisfaction score.
- Numa case studies showed CSI gains of 80→94, 820→981 after AI adoption.

### 11. Missed Call Rate
- Percentage of calls not answered.
- Typical dealership misses 300-500 calls per week.
- 75% of voicemail callers never call back.

### 12. Abandoned Call Rate
- Callers who hang up while on hold.
- Industry average: 31.8% hang up on hold.
